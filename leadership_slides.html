Leadership Style 0105 / 18
Authoritarian

Autocratic
Leadership

Top-down control — leader dictates strategy, policy, and tasks

One-way communication — instructions flow down, little to no input

High power distance — relies on position authority, not relationships

Works: Urgent situations. Risks: Micromanagement, dependency.
Examples: Martha Stewart · Bill Belichick05
Leadership Style 0206 / 18
Participative

Democratic
Leadership

Gathers input from team members — everyone contributes

Two-way communication — "What do you think?"

Low power distance — approachable, connects personally

Works: Creative teams. Risks: Slower decisions.
Examples: Bill Gates · Nelson Mandela · Walt Disney06
Leadership Style 0307 / 18
Hands-off

Laissez-faire
Leadership

Complete autonomy — followers make their own decisions

Leader provides resources but minimal supervision

Trust-based — effective when followers are highly skilled

Works: Expert teams. Risks: Ambiguity, lack of direction.
Examples: Warren Buffett · Ronald Reagan07

Theory14 / 18
Theory Meets Practice

What Makes Democratic
Leadership Work

Low Power Distance

The gap between leader and followers feels minimal. Democratic leaders are approachable and connect personally.

Two-Way Communication

Information flows both ways. Leaders seek input before deciding — "Here's what I'm thinking, what do you think?"

Shared Decision-Making

Leaders still make the final call, but delegate when possible. Followers have a real stake in the outcomes.

Accountability Remains

If a decision goes wrong, the democratic leader is still responsible. Authority shared, accountability never.

Why It Works

Risks

When It Fails

Decision paralysis — too many voices slow things down

Not all input is equal — unskilled teams struggle

Some may feel left out if their ideas aren't chosen

Requires strong facilitation skills from the leader
